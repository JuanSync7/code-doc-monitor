# Writing style: concise

A concise style says what is needed and stops. It respects the reader's time
without becoming cryptic. Distinct from reference-dense: it still reads as
sentences, it just refuses to pad them.

Guidance:

- Prefer the shorter word and the shorter sentence whenever meaning is
  preserved.
- Delete every word that can be removed without losing information or clarity.
- State the point once. Trust the reader; do not re-explain what you just said.
- Cut throat-clearing openers and redundant qualifiers ("basically",
  "in order to" -> "to").
- Use lists for three or more parallel items rather than a long sentence.
- Keep one example where it earns its space; drop a second that adds nothing.
- Conciseness is not omission: never drop a fact the reader needs to act
  correctly.
