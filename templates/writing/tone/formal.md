# Tone: formal

A formal tone is impersonal, measured, and suited to specifications, contracts,
and documents of record. It favours completeness and consistency over
approachability, and reads the same to every audience.

Guidance:

- Write in the third person; avoid "you" and "we". State what the system does,
  not what the reader should feel.
- Use full words rather than contractions ("do not" rather than "don't").
- Keep terminology consistent: choose one term for a concept and use it
  everywhere, matching the defined vocabulary.
- Prefer declarative statements of fact and requirement ("The loader rejects an
  unknown key").
- Where obligation matters, use precise modal language ("MUST", "MUST NOT",
  "MAY") consistently.
- Avoid rhetorical questions, asides, and humour.
- Maintain even, unemphatic phrasing; let structure, not exclamation, carry
  importance.
