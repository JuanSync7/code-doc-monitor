# Tone: precise

A precise tone prizes accuracy and unambiguity above warmth or flourish. The
reader should be able to act on a sentence without re-reading it. Every claim is
checkable against the code.

Guidance:

- Say exactly what is true and no more. Avoid hedges ("should usually",
  "probably") unless the uncertainty is real and worth stating.
- Use the exact identifier names, types, and values from the surface. Quote
  them in `code font`.
- Prefer concrete nouns and specific verbs over vague ones ("returns the parsed
  config" beats "handles the configuration").
- One idea per sentence. Split a sentence that carries two claims.
- State conditions explicitly: "when `timeout` is 0" not "in some cases".
- Avoid marketing adjectives ("powerful", "seamless", "robust"); they assert
  without informing.
- Where a behaviour has an edge, name the edge rather than glossing over it.
