# Document type: tutorial

A tutorial takes a newcomer by the hand and walks them from nothing to a first
working result. The reader is learning; they do not yet know the vocabulary or
the shape of the system. Success is measured by whether they reach the end with
something that works.

Guidance:

- Define one concrete, achievable goal up front and state what the reader will
  have built by the end.
- Order strictly by the steps the reader takes, not by how the system is
  structured. Each step builds on the previous one.
- Make every step do-able: show the exact command or code, then show the exact
  output to expect so the reader can confirm they are on track.
- Keep choices to a minimum. Decide for the reader; defer alternatives and
  edge cases to how-to guides and reference.
- Explain just enough theory to make the next step make sense — no more.
- Never leave a step that can fail silently; tell the reader how to recognise
  and recover from the common stumble.
- End with a working artifact and a clear pointer to what to read next.
