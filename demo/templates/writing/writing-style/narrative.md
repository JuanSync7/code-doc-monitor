# Writing style: narrative

A narrative style carries the reader through a connected line of reasoning. It
is built for understanding and retention: ideas arrive in an order that builds,
each one motivating the next. Best for explanations and conceptual guides.

Guidance:

- Establish context before detail. Tell the reader why this matters before how
  it works.
- Use transitions that signal the logical move ("because", "which means",
  "so that", "by contrast") so the thread is never lost.
- Develop one idea per paragraph, with a topic sentence that states it and the
  rest that supports it.
- Introduce a concept before you rely on it; do not reference what the reader
  has not yet met.
- Use full sentences and flowing paragraphs rather than dense bullet lists.
- Bring in concrete examples and analogies to anchor abstract points.
- Close the loop: return at the end to the question or motivation you opened
  with.
